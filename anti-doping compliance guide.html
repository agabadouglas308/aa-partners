Your Guide to Clean Sport in Uganda

This comprehensive guide outlines the essential anti-doping regulations and compliance requirements for Ugandan athletes competing at all levels. Understanding these rules is crucial to protect your career, reputation, and the integrity of Ugandan sports.

The Governing Bodies

Ugandan athletes are subject to multiple layers of anti-doping governance:

  • World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA): Sets the global rules through the World Anti-Doping Code.

Key Compliance Areas

1. The Prohibited List

Rule: You are 100% responsible for any substance found in your body (strict liability).

Action: Check the WADA Prohibited List annually (updated every January 1). It lists all banned substances and methods in- and out-of-competition.

Medication Safety Checklist

2. Therapeutic Use Exemption (TUE)

Rule: If you need to use a prohibited medication for a legitimate medical condition, you must apply for a TUE BEFORE use (except emergencies).

Process:

  1. Consult your doctor about the necessity of the medication
  2. Apply through the UNADO TUE Committee for national-level competition
  3. For international competitions, your International Federation may require approval

3. Whereabouts & Testing

Rule: Registered Testing Pool (RTP) athletes must provide detailed whereabouts for out-of-competition testing.

Requirements: Quarterly filing specifying a 60-minute daily time slot, overnight location, training venue, and competition schedule.

Consequence: Three whereabouts failures within 12 months = an automatic Anti-Doping Rule Violation (2-year ban potential).

Specific Risks for Ugandan Athletes

Traditional Medicines & Herbal Supplements: These are high-risk. They may contain undeclared, banned substances. Use only batch-tested supplements from reliable sources, or avoid them entirely.

Education Gap: Many athletes are unaware of all compliance requirements. Actively participate in UNADO's anti-doping education programs.
